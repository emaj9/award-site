Welcome

Welcome to the CSUS Sustainable Teaching Award Website!

What is this award?

This student-led award rewards instructors who teach computer science (CS) in a way that acknowledges the social and environmental impacts of computing. In creating this award, we hope to promote the integration of ethics, sustainability, and social justice in computer science (CS) education at McGill.

Why is this award necessary?

CS is taught in an immaterial way, lacking focus on the intersecting ethical, environmental, and social justice implications of computing. These socially disconnected teaching practices lead students to abstract the world around them in a way that only allows them to address symptoms of larger socio-political issues but not the root causes. Providing students with a full picture of computing is necessary for CS graduates to effectively leverage CS in their solutions to real-world issues. This means situating CS in its social and historical context, and acknowledging the environmental and human costs of computing. We believe that incorporating these issues into CS education will also improve studentsâ€™ understanding of course material. Moreover, we hope that promoting a socially grounded curriculum will help make CS less harmful to people and the environment.
